How to Write a Professional Resignation Email

Best Practices for Resigning

Resigning from a job is a significant professional step. Follow these guidelines to ensure a smooth transition:

1. Timing is Important

Submit your resignation during business hours, ideally at the beginning or middle of the week. Give at least two weeks' notice unless your contract specifies otherwise.

2. Keep it Professional

Regardless of your reasons for leaving, maintain a professional and positive tone. Avoid negative comments about the company, colleagues, or management.

3. Offer Transition Help

Show your professionalism by offering to help with the transition. This can include training your replacement or documenting your processes.

4. Send to the Right People

Send your resignation email to your direct manager and HR department. Consider having a conversation with your manager before sending the formal email.

Resignation Email FAQs

How much notice should I give when resigning? +

In the United States, the standard notice period is two weeks. However, check your employment contract for any specific requirements. For senior positions, 3-4 weeks may be more appropriate.

Should I explain why I'm resigning? +

You're not required to provide detailed reasons. A general statement like "pursuing new opportunities" or "personal reasons" is usually sufficient. Only share more if you're comfortable and it's positive.

What should the subject line be? +

Use a clear, professional subject line like "Resignation - [Your Name]" or "Notice of Resignation - [Your Position]". This ensures your email is easily identifiable.

Should I resign in person or by email? +

Ideally, have a conversation with your manager first, then follow up with a formal resignation email. If an in-person conversation isn't possible, a phone call followed by email is acceptable.

What if I'm asked to leave immediately? +

Some companies have policies where employees in certain positions are asked to leave immediately upon resignation. This is often for security reasons and typically includes pay for the notice period.
